Cheap IP cameras are widely available online for very little money – and at first glance, they look identical to professional equipment. But appearances can be deceiving. For businesses that want to protect their infrastructure, staff and assets, the difference between consumer and professional devices is significant – in reliability, security and real-world value.

## The Problem with Cheap Consumer Cameras

### Cloud Dependency and Privacy Risks

Most cheap IP cameras store recordings on the manufacturer's cloud – often on servers outside Switzerland or the EU. This raises serious data protection questions: who can access your camera footage? How long is it stored? What happens if the provider shuts down their service?

In a business context, this is a real problem. Both Switzerland's Data Protection Act (revDSG) and the GDPR require that personal data (and video recordings are personal data) is stored securely and traceably.

### Limited Integration Possibilities

Consumer cameras are standalone solutions. Every manufacturer has its own app, its own portal, its own cloud. Running multiple brands means juggling multiple interfaces with no central overview.

### Software Quality and Security Updates

Numerous studies have shown that cheap IP cameras often ship with critical security vulnerabilities – and regular firmware updates frequently never arrive. A compromised device can serve as an entry point for attacks on the entire company network.

## When Does the Switch Make Sense?

UniFi Protect pays off for businesses from around 3–4 cameras – at that point, the benefits of centralised management and local data storage clearly outweigh the higher upfront cost compared to individual consumer devices. For businesses in regulated sectors (healthcare, finance, law), a professional solution with demonstrable GDPR compliance is often a requirement rather than a choice.

## Conclusion

Cheap consumer cameras are an acceptable solution for home use. In a business environment they quickly reach their limits – in terms of data protection, reliability and integration. UniFi Protect provides a professional, scalable and privacy-compliant alternative that grows with your organisation.
